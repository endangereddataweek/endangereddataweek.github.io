Recent political events in the US have shed new light on the fragility of federally produced data. Endangered Data Week is a collaborative effort, coordinated across campuses, nonprofits, libraries, citizen science initiatives, and cultural heritage institutions, to shed light on public datasets that are in danger of being deleted, repressed, or lost. This week's events can amplify attention to endangered collections by publicizing the availability of datasets; visualizing and increasing critical engagement with them; encouraging political activism for open data policies; and fostering data skills through workshops on curation, documentation, improved access, and preservation.
